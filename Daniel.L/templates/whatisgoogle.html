What is Google?

Overview
Google is a global technology company best known for its search engine. Founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin, Google has grown to offer a wide range of products and services, including advertising, cloud computing, software, and hardware.
History
Google started as a research project at Stanford University. Its innovative PageRank algorithm revolutionized web search. Over the years, Google has expanded through acquisitions and the development of new technologies, becoming one of the world’s most influential companies.
Mission and Values
Google's mission is "to organize the world’s information and make it universally accessible and useful." The company emphasizes innovation, user focus, and making a positive impact on society.
Business Model
Google generates most of its revenue from advertising through its AdWords and AdSense platforms. It also earns income from cloud services, hardware, and app sales.
Global Impact
Google has transformed how people access and use information, communicate, and do business. Its products and services are used by billions worldwide, shaping the digital landscape and influencing technology trends.
