These are some basic skills for saxophonist


Learning the saxophone as a beginner starts with mastering a few essential skills. Breath control is key—it helps produce a smooth, steady tone. You’ll also focus on forming a proper embouchure, which is how you shape your mouth and lips around the mouthpiece. Finger placement is another core skill; getting used to the key layout takes time but builds a solid foundation for playing notes and scales. Lastly, developing a good ear by listening and matching pitch will sharpen your musicality early on. With patience and consistent practice, these basics pave the way for expressive, confident playing.

Take some notes from the greats

People like Charlie Parker, John Coltrane, Sonny Rollins, Coleman Hawkins, and Stan Getz

Charlie Parker, often called “Bird,” revolutionized jazz with his groundbreaking contributions to the bebop movement. His virtuosic alto saxophone playing introduced lightning-fast improvisation, complex harmonies, and a new rhythmic freedom that reshaped the genre’s possibilities. Alongside contemporaries like Dizzy Gillespie and Max Roach, Parker pushed jazz from danceable swing into a more cerebral, expressive art form. His influence extended beyond technique—he inspired generations of musicians to explore personal expression through improvisation and to treat jazz as a serious, evolving musical language. Even decades after his passing, Parker’s legacy continues to echo through modern jazz and beyond
